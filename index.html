Background

Zachary Langford recently completed his PhD in Energy Science and Engineering from the University of Tennessee, a joint program with Oak Ridge National Laboratory (ORNL). His PhD research at ORNL applied multivariate data mining and machine learning methodologies to aerial imagery and environmental datasets for landscape characterization in Arctic ecosystems.

Research Interests

His primary research interests consist of:

  • identifying characteristics from a variety of sensing modalities (multi/hyperspectral, thermal, etc.),

  • spatio-temporal statistics and geospatial applications, and

  • data mining applications in scientific disciplines such as environmental sciences, cyber-physical systems, and intelligence analysis.
